HOT TUB REMOVAL — Q&A

Do I need to drain the hot tub before you arrive?

Yes — please drain the hot tub and disconnect power before we arrive. If you need help with this, a plumber or electrician can assist. Once it's drained and disconnected, we handle everything else from there.

How do you remove a hot tub from a backyard with limited access?

We're experienced with tight access situations — side gates, fences, and narrow pathways. We break the hot tub down into manageable sections on-site using power tools, then carry each piece out. We protect your landscaping and surfaces during the process.

How much does hot tub removal cost in Bakersfield?

Hot tub removal typically runs $250–$500 depending on size, access difficulty, and whether a deck or pad needs to be removed as well. Text us a photo of the tub and access point for an accurate quote.

How long does hot tub removal take?

Most hot tub removals take 2–3 hours from arrival to final cleanup. Larger units or those in difficult locations may take a bit longer. We'll give you a realistic time estimate when we review your project.

Do you also remove the concrete pad or deck beneath the hot tub?

We can remove wooden decking and structures around the hot tub. For concrete pad removal, let us know in advance so we can bring the right equipment and adjust the quote accordingly.

Where does the old hot tub go?

Fiberglass and acrylic components go to appropriate waste facilities. Metal and mechanical parts are recycled where possible. We don't just dump — we dispose responsibly.
